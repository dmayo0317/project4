Digital Currency

The Invisible Coins of the Future

What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital currency that uses a decentralized system to verify and maintain records of transactions. These facts make crypto almost impossible to counterfeit or double spend. Crypto is based on blockchain technology. Blockchain is the system that keeps the record of transactions maintained across several computers that are linked in a peer-to-peer network. Basically, think about crypto as the digital equivalent to tickets at a carnival to ride a Ferris wheel or play a game. Crypto can be either purchased online or the coins can be “mined”. Mining crypto refers to solving complex mathematical problems and being rewarded with either a coin or parts of a coin.

Cryptocurrency Timeline

While crypto’s popularity has dramatically increased over the past ten years, it was actually started in 1983 by David Chaum. Chaum created the first cryptocurrency called eCash. Although the term cryptocurrency wasn’t coined until 1990, this first digital currency was similar to today’s crypto because the transactions were anonymous and conducted over a public network. Of course crypto did not become popular until 2008, when the value of gold and coins fell dramatically. At this time was when an individual named Satoshi Nakamoto conducted the first transaction with the most well-known cryptocurrency to this day: Bitcoin. Bitcoin soon established itself as the ruler of cryptocurrency and its value skyrocketed to $10,000 in 2017. In September of 2021, El Salvador made history and became the first country to adopt Bitcoin as official currency.

How Many Cryptocurrencies are there?

Let’s just say a lot. There are hundreds of types of crypto that are used in daily transactions. These crypto’s include those made by everyday companies such as Tesla, Netflix or Google but also include crypto created by individuals such as Dogecoin, Ethereum, or Solana. Even certain countries are creating their own cryptos and establishing them as their own national currency. This includes Venezuela with Petro, Dubai(UAE) with Emcash, and Estonia with Estcoin. Needless to say that there are a huge number of cryptos on the market.
